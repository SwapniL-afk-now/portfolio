About

A bit about me, properly.

A short version is on the first page. Here's the longer one — who I am, what I've been doing, and what I'm looking for.

I graduated in February 2025 with a B.Sc. in Electrical & Electronic Engineering from BUET, where I was a University Merit Scholarship holder and on the Dean's List (CGPA 3.73/4.0). My undergraduate research — done under Prof. Mohammad Ariful Haque — focused on building vision-language models for dermatology in low-resource settings. That work produced my first two preprints (CLARIFY and GRPO++) and earned the 1st Runner-Up at the BUET EEE Bear Summit 2024 poster competition.

After graduating I joined Advanced Chemical Industries (ACI) as a Machine Learning Engineer, and in November 2025 began a remote research collaboration with Prof. Ser-Nam Lim at the University of Central Florida. With Prof. Lim I'm working on policy-gradient methods for LLM reasoning — this is where GXPO came from.

My research sits at the intersection of reinforcement learning, multimodal learning, and optimization. The throughline is simple: I want language models that are more stable to train, cheaper to run, and better at structured reasoning — especially when the data is small, the domain is medical, or the inference budget is tight. The bulk of my work is open-source, so if any of it is useful to you, please steal it.

"I'm looking for a PhD where I can spend four years thinking hard about why policy-gradient methods work the way they do, and what that implies for multimodal reasoning."

Outside of research I co-organize a small ML reading group, mentor undergrads from BUET, and write occasionally about things I've learned the hard way. I run on strong coffee, long walks, and the quiet pleasure of a passing unit test.

    Research

    Three threads, one direction.

    I work on problems where optimization, multimodality, and reasoning meet. The goal is the same in every thread: build models that learn reliably from limited signal and deploy cheaply in the real world.

    01 / 03

    Reinforcement learning for reasoning models

    Policy gradient · Optimization · Preference alignment

    GRPO and its variants are the workhorse of modern RL-for-reasoning, but they have well-known failure modes: unstable training, mode collapse, and over- reliance on single-step gradients. I work on two specific remedies. GXPO is a plug-compatible policy-update rule for GRPO that approximates a longer local lookahead using three backward passes, then takes a corrective step at the predicted position. On Qwen2.5 and Llama math-reasoning benchmarks it adds +1.65 to +5.00 average pass@1 over GRPO at the same active-phase cost. GRPO++ stabilizes the same loss for low-resource VLM fine-tuning, then aligns the resulting model with a knowledge-graph-based DPO step. The broader question driving this thread is: what does it take to make policy-gradient RL converge reliably when the rollout budget is small and the reward is noisy?

    GRPO / GRPO++ GXPO (lookahead policy extrapolation) DPO with KG-derived preferences Convergence stability & sample efficiency
    02 / 03

    Multimodal vision-language models

    VLM design · Compression · Grounded reasoning

    A vision-language model is only useful if it can run where the user is and reason about what it sees. I work on the full arc of that problem — from architecture to deployment. CLARIFY is a Specialist–Generalist framework: a small, accurate classifier (the Specialist) drives the reasoning of a compressed conversational VLM (the Generalist), grounded by a knowledge-graph retriever. It lifts diagnostic accuracy by 18% over the strongest single-model baseline while cutting VRAM by 20% and latency by 5%. Compression Strategies shows a prune → SFT → quantize pipeline that gets a LLaVA-7B medical VLM down to 4 GB of VRAM (a 70% reduction) with a 4% performance gain. DermIQ-VLM (the GRPO++ paper) adds RL-driven reasoning to the same loop. The medical setting is the testbed; the goal is general-purpose VLMs that are accurate, compressed, and grounded in external knowledge.

    Specialist–Generalist routing Structural pruning + activation-aware quantization Knowledge-graph grounded VQA RL-aligned medical VLM
    03 / 03

    Agents & efficiency

    Tool use · Long-horizon reasoning · Cost-aware deployment

    The third thread is about giving models the right tools and the right budget. In GVD (graph-discovery), an agent doesn't process a long video in one pass — it builds a typed Video Knowledge Graph once (Whisper + OCR + VLM scene extraction), then traverses it through graph-native tools: multi-hop traversal along CAUSAL · ENTITY · TEMPORAL · SPEAKER · SIMILAR edges, causal reasoning, and entity timeline queries. The companion Knowledge_Graph_with_LLM project is the extraction half: a DeepSeek-distilled model that pulls (entity, relation, entity) triples out of text into a NetworkX graph. Bangla OCR, AgroResearch AI, and the AI Voice Sales Agent push the same efficiency-first philosophy into applied settings — small models, on-device runtimes (ONNX, 4-bit quant), and explicit cost budgets at inference time.

    ReAct-style tool use Long-video understanding LLM triple extraction ONNX & 4-bit quantized deployment
